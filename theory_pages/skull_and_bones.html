Theory Related to the Skull and Bones

Contents

Section 1: Function and composition

  • Support the facial structures (facial skull)
  • Protect the brain (cranium)

Composition

  • There are 29 bones associated with the skull:
    • 8 cranial bones
    • 14 facial bones
    • 1 hyoid (associated with the pharynx)
    • 6 Auditory bones (ossicles, 3x each side)
  • The cranial bones exist in either a paired or singular configuration:
    • Paired: Temporal, Parietal
    • Single: Frontal, Occipital, Ethmoid, Sphenoid
  • The same can be said for the facial bones:
    • Paired: Maxilla, Zygomatic, Lacrimal, Nasal, Inferior Nasal Concha, Palatine
    • Single: Vomer, Mandible

Section 2: General features of the Skull

Articulations of the skull are mostly non-moveable sutures, which are fibrous joints that may ossify with age.

The exception is the ossicles of the ear and the Temporomandibular Joint (TMJ)

Section 3: The Frontal Bone

  • Description:

    A large bone visible anteriorly and laterally on the skull. Lays under the forehead and protects the front of the head.

  • Attachments:

    [TBD]

  • Notable features:

    • Glabella: A small prominence at the front of the glabella
    • Supraorbital notch/Foramen: A hole/notch for the supraorbital nerve, artery and vein to exit.
    • Orbital surface: The surface of the frontal bone visible from inside the eye socket
    • Nasion: attachment point between the frontal and nasal bones
    • Zygomatic process: extension from the frontal bone to the zygomatic bone
    • Sutures: Key sutures include the coronal suture.

Section 4: The Nasal Bone

  • Description:

    A paired set of bones that make up the most superior part of the external nose.

  • Attachments:

    Attaches to the nasal part of frontal bone, the maxilla (via nasomaxillary suture), and the cartilage of the nose.

  • Notable features:

    Nasion: The attachment point between the frontal and nasal bones
